Further Analysis into Hospitalizations¶

Let's take our analysis to the next level. Let's look at the hospitalizations due to COVID and compare them to case numbers. Hopefully, this will be a better way to view the virus becuase of the issues with cases, due to limited testing. I have a hypothesis that a lower percentage of cases are being hospitalized. The motivation for this hypothesis is that more cases (and less severe cases) are being caught with an increase in testing, as well as possible better at home treatments, or the hospitals being able to discharge quicker with better treatments. To examine all of this, first let's look at the number of hospitalizations, number in ICU, and number on ventaliators

Conclusion and Further Reading¶

We have now examined many angles of the COVID spread in the United States. We have gone over a very informative data source that has plenty of ways to examine data. We have looked at different factors that may be hindering our ability to gather accurate data. Despite all of that, we have been able to answer some questions. Is COVID getting worse? Yes and no. COVID is spreading around the nation. We have seen huge spikes in number of cases, as well as % positive that reflects expanded testing capabilities. We have also seen hospitalizations and deaths increase, which show in many different ways that COVID is spreading once again. It is also not neccessarily getting worse. A lower number are being hospitalized, and a much lower number are dying. There are some treatments (attached below) that have helped survival rates. Hospitals are now slightly better equipped to treat and doctors have better intuition as to what works. At the end of the day, the numbers are still very high.
